How does it work?

This app runs by analyzing images in the Yahoo Flickr Creative Commons 100 Million photo dataset as well as the most recent photos uploaded by Flickr users. Once the photos are obtained, they are first filtered so that only those containing the hashtag #painting and with GPS metadata are kept.
The style in each image is classified via a deep learning technique utilizing a Convolutional Neural Network.





What does it give?

The neural network has been trained on 80 thousand images to classify the style of different pictures. It analyzes the pixels in the image and their surrounding pixels to determine important features that are most descriptive of the image's style and then assigns probabilities to each of 20 style classes. The one related to optical techniques: Macro, Bokeh, Depth-of-Field, Long Exposure, HDR are not of interest here.

Deep Dreams (with Caffe)

Instead of exactly prescribing which feature the network has to amplify, we can also let the network make that decision. In this case it is simply fed with an arbitrary image and is free to analyze the picture. We just pick a layer and ask the network to enhance whatever it detected. Each layer of the network deals with features at a different level of abstraction, so the complexity of generated features depends on which layer we choose to enhance. For example, lower layers tend to produce simple ornament-like patterns, because those layers are sensitive to basic features such as edges and their orientations turning your picture into an Impressionism style!

More dreams...

If we choose higher-level layers, which identify more sophisticated features in images, complex features or even whole objects tend to emerge. Again, we just start with an existing image and give it to our neural net. The network is basically asked: Whatever you see there, we want more of it! This creates a feedback loop: if a cloud looks a little bit like a bird, the network will make it look more like a bird. This in turn will make the network recognize the bird even more strongly on the next pass and so forth, until a highly detailed bird appears, seemingly out of nowhere turning your picture into a Surrealism style!
